Course Description

ARTS 173 is a course in the effective visual presentation of ideas for the web, from concept and design to structure, preparation, and production. This course focuses on the elements and principles of design as applied to layout, graphics, and animation.

Requisites

Minimum 2.5 in ARTS 171 or concurrently and Reading Level 5 and Writing Level 6.

Course Note: Adobe Dreamweaver software is used. Online students are required to have semester-long access to Adobe Dreamweaver CS6 or CC. Students have access to these in the LCC TLC 100 Computer Lab, or in the Computer Graphics Labs in the Gannon Building.

Outcomes

Learning Outcomes

(DEPARTMENT SPECIFIED STUDENT PERFORMANCE OBJECTIVES)

Based upon industry standards and professional practices, the successful student will be able to:

  1. Explain the requirements and limitations for the on-screen presentation of ideas.
  2. Create a web page based on elements and principles of design.
  3. Determine design priorities and alternatives based on an understanding of clients' requirements and message.
  4. Re-purpose text and graphics from various sources.
  5. Prepare images for the web.
  6. Add multimedia elements and effects to web sites.
  7. Use server side technology to parse user input.
  8. Create simple animations for the web.
  9. Communicate design solutions visually and verbally.
  10. Explain basic CSS concepts visually and verbally.

Plagiarism Policy

Plagiarism is a cardinal subject in art. Fair use will be covered in some detail throughout the class. Failing to understand fair use — intentionally or not — can cost a creative company or individual millions of dollars. This is why LCC’s goal is to correct plagiarism and unfair use early on.

Plagiarized assignments will be scored as zero with no chance to make up the assignment—whether the cause was malice or ignorance. You will lose all participation points for the day. In certain cases, particularly multiple offenses, it may be reported to the LCC Behavior Intervention Team.

If ever in doubt, draw it or shoot it yourself.
